V2 thesis — what changed from the rejected board

v1 dressed the phone in the web costume and read as Substack. v2 inverts the seam: the application body is Apple’s material at Apple’s geometry — grouped cells, stock buttons, untouched dialogs, system type — and Nuke appears only where the data is: mono digits, square thumbnails, the barcode, and one signature artifact per surface (the receipt card). The web keeps the full square canon; the phone borrows legitimacy until the mark earns its own. Codex amendments proposed in CONSTRAINTS.md.

Copy budget: F1 carries 13 words, F2 carries 13 inside our cells. No frame exists without a named working organ. The storyboard now begins one frame before the app does, because the convincing does too.

## Measurement Procedure

1. **Start at the ECU/PDM connector location** on the firewall (driver side)
2. **Route the tape measure along the actual path** the wire will take — follow existing harness channels, frame rails, or the intended loom routing
3. **Add 12 inches of service loop** (6 inches at each end) — this is NOT included in the estimated lengths
4. **Record the measured length** in the MEASURED column (include the service loop)
5. **Note any obstacles** — tight bends, heat sources (exhaust manifold, headers), moving parts, sharp edges
6. **Record the routing path** in shorthand (e.g., "FW grommet > valve cover > cyl 3")
7. **Calculate variance** after all measurements: (Measured - Estimated) / Estimated x 100

### Engine Loom Measurement Notes
- Passenger-side coils and injectors (even cylinders) require crossing over the intake manifold — expect 6-12 inches longer than driver-side wires
- Shielded cables (CKP, CMP, KS1, KS2) must route AWAY from ignition coil wires and exhaust/headers
- CKP sensor is at the rear of the block near the bellhousing — this is the longest engine sensor run
- Knock sensors are below the exhaust manifold — route wires to avoid direct heat contact
- All engine wires share a common trunk from the firewall grommet to the first branch point at the front of the valve covers

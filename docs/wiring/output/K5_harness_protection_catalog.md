## 1. DR-25 HEAT SHRINK TUBING

**Raychem DR-25** -- the backbone of Professional-tier harness protection. 2:1 shrink ratio, 150C continuous / 175C short term. Chemical resistant, abrasion resistant, halogen-free. This is what separates a professional loom from a zip-tie-and-tape job.

### Sizing Rule
DR-25 shrinks 2:1. Select the size where the RECOVERED (shrunk) ID is slightly smaller than the bundle OD, and the SUPPLIED (unshrunk) ID fits over the bundle and connectors.

### Field-Formed Boot Method (Professional Tier)

Instead of buying expensive Raychem molded boots ($15-40 each), Professional tier typically field-forms boots:
1. Slide DR-25 over the trunk section
2. At the branch point, overlap a piece of SCL adhesive heat shrink that covers the junction
3. Heat the SCL first (seals the junction), then heat the DR-25 trunk sections
4. The SCL adhesive bonds to the DR-25, creating a waterproof Y-transition

## APPENDIX B: CONNECTOR TERMINATION SEAL METHOD

Every crimp joint follows this sequence:

1. Strip wire to correct length for the terminal
2. Slide correct-size dual-wall adhesive heat shrink over the wire BEFORE crimping
3. Crimp terminal with correct tool (Daniels indent or quality ratchet)
4. Pull-test the crimp (10 lb minimum for 22 AWG, 20 lb for 18 AWG, 30 lb for 14 AWG)
5. Slide the dual-wall heat shrink over the crimp barrel, covering the barrel and 1/4" of insulation
6. Heat with calibrated heat gun (start at the adhesive end, work outward)
7. Verify adhesive squeeze-out at both ends of the heat shrink

For sensor pigtail transitions (harness wire to pigtail wire):
1. Butt-splice or solder the junction
2. Slide SCL adhesive heat shrink over the junction
3. Heat to seal
4. The SCL creates a waterproof transition from the harness to the OEM pigtail

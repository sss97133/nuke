# Luxe Fleet — Vehicle Asset Notes

## THE BRONCO ROADSTER CONCEPT (Game Changer)

Ford built a one-of-a-kind Bronco Roadster Concept, unveiled August 16, 2025 at Silver Lake for Bronco's 60th anniversary. Designed by Robert Gelardi (Bronco Chief Designer).

**What it is:** A modern 6th-gen (6G) Bronco 2-door stripped back and restyled as the original 1966 U13 Roadster. Half-cab, completely open air, bench seat, fold-down tailgate with stamped FORD wordmark, Fifteen52 Analog wheels, Wimbledon White paint, chrome-style bumpers.

**Key design philosophy:** "Addition by subtraction" — they took the modern Bronco and asked "what can we take away?" to recapture the "fun through simplicity" that made the original compelling. The brief for the 1966 Bronco was literally "make a Mustang for off-road."

**Status:** One-of-a-kind concept. "Not intended for production." BUT it physically exists. It was built on the 6G platform.

**Luxe Fleet angle:** Ask Ford to PLACE the Roadster Concept at Fouquet's for a season. It's sitting in a warehouse somewhere. On St. Barths it would be the most photographed vehicle on the island. The "OG to 6G" story told in the most exclusive setting in the Caribbean.

## Critical Distinction: Bronco vs Bronco Sport

| | Full-Size Bronco | Bronco Sport |
|--|-----------------|-------------|
| Type | Body-on-frame SUV | Unibody compact crossover |
| Doors | 2-door or 4-door | 4-door only |
| Removable doors | YES | NO |
| Removable roof | YES (hard top or soft top) | NO |
| Open-air driving | YES — the 2-door with doors off is the iconic image | NO — it's a closed crossover |
| Width | 75.9" (1,928mm) — WIDE for SBH roads | 74.3" (1,887mm) — slightly narrower |
| Heritage Edition | YES — $59,500, retro graphics, special colors | NO Heritage Edition trim |
| Price | $37,000-62,000 depending on trim | $30,000-38,000 |
| SBH road suitability | Challenging — wide, tall, heavy | Better — more car-like, easier parking |
| Island sell-through | Premium price, niche buyer | Easy sell — rental fleets, daily drivers |

## What This Means for Luxe Fleet

**The guest fleet (Fouquet's buys):** Full-size Bronco, Heritage Edition. 2-door preferred for the open-air experience. This is the product guests fall in love with. Doors off, roof off, driving to Gouverneur Beach.

**The publicity vehicle (Ford sends):** Full-size Bronco 2-door makes better photos than Bronco Sport. The Sport is a crossover — it doesn't have the open-air drama. BUT the Sport is the one that sells to rental fleets.

**The rental fleet pipeline:** Bronco Sport. Easier to drive, park, and maintain. Rental operators want the Sport, not the full-size. This is the volume play.

**Recommendation:** The deck should feature BOTH:
- Full-size Bronco 2-door (Heritage Edition, doors off) = the hero image, the guest experience, the emotional sell
- Bronco Sport = the practical vehicle, the rental fleet pipeline, the volume business case for Ford

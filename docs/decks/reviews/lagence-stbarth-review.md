# Fleet Luxe v9 -- Review from L'Agence St Barth

**Reviewer:** L'Agence St Barth (Agency Principal perspective)
**Deck version:** v9
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Verdict:** Not ready to show to our network. Fixable, but needs real work.

---

## 1. WHAT'S MISSING (Dealbreakers)

### The deck has no ask. That is the single biggest problem.

We read 16 slides and still do not know: what are you asking L'Agence to do? What are you asking Ford to do? What are you asking Fouquet's to do? The deck describes a system but never says "here is what we need from you, here is what you get, here is the timeline." Every partner in this chain will ask the same question in the first 30 seconds: "What do you want from me and what do I get?" The deck has no answer.

### Specific gaps our clients will ask about:

- **Who owns the vehicles?** The economics slide shows two options but never resolves which one is proposed. "Option A or B" is not a pitch -- it is a homework assignment for the reader. Pick one. Present the other as upside.
- **What does the guest pay?** Is the Bronco included in the room rate? Is it a paid add-on? Is it complimentary for suite-level bookings? A Fouquet's guest paying EUR 3,000/night will not tolerate ambiguity about whether a vehicle costs extra.
- **What is the insurance and liability model?** One slide mentions "FBM sources local fleet policies." That is not a liability model. What happens when a guest puts a Heritage Edition into a wall on the road to Colombier? Who pays? What is the deductible? Is there a damage waiver? This is not a detail -- it is a dealbreaker for any hotel GM.
- **What is L'Agence's role, specifically?** Slide 05 says "PR, curation, client relationships. Ensures every touchpoint meets luxury standard." That is a sentence from a LinkedIn bio, not a scope of work. What touchpoints? Which clients? What does "curation" mean in operational terms? We cannot sign a partnership based on adjectives.
- **What is L'Agence's compensation?** Not mentioned anywhere. We are not a charity. If you want us to open our client book and put our reputation behind this, there is a fee structure, an exclusivity arrangement, and a set of deliverables. None of that is in this deck.
- **What is the timeline?** The deck mentions "high season Nov-Apr" and "vehicles arrive by October" but there is no project timeline. When does Ford need to commit? When does the first vehicle ship? When is the launch event? Without dates, this is a concept, not a plan.
- **Who is the decision-maker at Ford?** "Ford Brand Team" is mentioned repeatedly but never named. Is this Ford Motor Company corporate? Ford of Europe? A regional marketing director? The Bronco brand manager? We need to know who we are actually pitching to.
- **What about Barriere Group?** Fouquet's St Barth is a Barriere property. The deck never mentions Barriere. Any real partnership goes through Barriere corporate in Paris, not just the local GM. This is a fundamental misunderstanding of how luxury hotel groups operate.

## 2. EDITORIAL VOICE CRITIQUE

### What feels right:

- The problem statement on Slide 01 is strong. The juxtaposition of a EUR 3,000/night experience with a Suzuki Jimny is visceral and true. Every concierge on this island has heard this complaint.
- "The vehicle is the one piece of the luxury promise nobody owns" -- that is a real insight. Keep it.
- The supply chain detail (Slides 10-12) shows genuine homework. The octroi de mer, carte grise, SOGUAVA bottleneck -- this is not surface-level. It signals operational seriousness.

### What feels off:

- **"From landing to Instagram" (Slide 09)** -- This is the language of a brand activation agency pitching to a CMO, not the language of luxury. Our clients do not think in terms of "content value" and "social amplification." They think in terms of experience, exclusivity, and taste. The Instagram reference cheapens the entire proposition. A guest at Fouquet's does not need to be told their experience will end up on Instagram. That is implied. Saying it out loud makes this feel like an influencer campaign.
- **"Every vehicle is content" (Slide 08)** -- Same problem. This is marketing-speak. A Heritage Edition Bronco parked at a villa on Shell Beach is not "content." It is an object in a landscape. The content happens naturally if the object is beautiful enough. Saying "every vehicle is content" reveals that the presenter is thinking about this from Ford's perspective (impressions, reach, ROI) rather than from the guest's perspective (beauty, pleasure, freedom).
- **"Ford sees their 2026 Heritage fleet in daily use" (Slide 06)** -- Why does the guest care what Ford sees? This feedback loop is relevant for a Ford pitch deck, not for a luxury hospitality pitch. The guest journey should end with the guest, not with Ford's marketing department.
- **"Zero content value" (Slides 01, 08) as a criticism of current vehicles** -- Again, content-value framing. The problem with a dented Moke is not that it has "zero content value." The problem is that it is ugly, uncomfortable, and inconsistent with the rest of the experience. Frame it in experiential terms, not media terms.
- **The tone oscillates between luxury hospitality and startup pitch deck.** Phrases like "The operational backbone," "Proven paperwork," "VLVA (NV dealer, DLR000053625)" -- these are internal operations details that should not appear in a client-facing or partner-facing document. They belong in an appendix or a separate operations memo.

### Would we show this to a EUR 50M net worth individual?

No. Not in its current form. The operational detail is impressive but it overwhelms the emotional proposition. A UHNW individual does not want to see dealer license numbers, tax form references, or freight routing. They want to see: here is something beautiful, here is why it belongs here, here is what it feels like. The operational proof should exist but should be available on request, not in the primary narrative.

## 3. BRAND HIERARCHY ISSUES

### Ford is too prominent.

The cover slide has a Ford logo that is 1.5x the size of every other brand logo. The first vehicle-specific slide (03) is branded with a Ford blue accent stripe. The flow diagrams start with Ford. The guest journey ends with "Ford sees it."

This reads as a Ford activation deck that happens to take place at a hotel, not as a luxury experience that happens to feature Ford vehicles.

**The hierarchy should be:**
1. The experience (Fleet Luxe as concept)
2. The destination (St. Barths, the landscape, the lifestyle)
3. The hotel (Fouquet's as the venue)
4. The vehicle (Heritage Edition Bronco as the object)
5. The automaker (Ford as the enabler)

Ford should be last in the brand hierarchy, not first. The guest does not care that Ford made the vehicle. They care that the vehicle is beautiful, capable, and exclusive. If the Heritage Edition Bronco is good enough, the Ford association becomes a feature. If Ford is the headline, it feels like a sponsored placement -- which is exactly what luxury clients will reject.

### The tension between activation and experience:

There is an irreconcilable tension in this deck: it needs Ford's money (or at minimum Ford's cooperation) but it cannot feel like a Ford program. The way to resolve this is to build two versions of the deck:

- **Version A (for Ford):** Lead with brand activation, Caribbean market penetration, demographic expansion (the "70% male" stat), content impressions, dealer channel innovation. Ford loves this language.
- **Version B (for hospitality + L'Agence network):** Lead with the guest experience, the aesthetic proposition, the competitive differentiation for the hotel. Ford is mentioned only as the vehicle provider, not as a partner or brand presence.

Right now, v9 is trying to be both and succeeding at neither.

## 5. CLIENT EXPERIENCE GAPS

### What happens when a guest damages a Bronco?

This is not hypothetical. The roads to Colombier, Gouverneur, and Grand Fond are steep, narrow, and unpaved in sections. Guests will scrape, dent, and possibly wreck these vehicles. The deck must address:
- Damage waiver policy (included in rate? separate charge?)
- Repair turnaround (FBM can handle minor work, but what about major body damage?)
- Replacement vehicle availability (if one Bronco is in the shop, does the fleet shrink to three?)
- Guest liability caps
- Insurance claim process

### What happens during off-season (May-October)?

The island population drops from 35,000 to 9,000. Most luxury hotels close or run skeleton operations. Fouquet's (as a Barriere property) likely operates at reduced capacity. What happens to the Broncos for 6 months? Storage costs? Maintenance during dormancy? Could they rotate to another hemisphere (Mediterranean summer season)? This affects the economics significantly -- the vehicles sit idle for half the year.

### Is the vehicle complimentary or a paid add-on?

This must be resolved before any hotel conversation:
- **Complimentary with suite booking:** Positions as amenity. Hotel absorbs cost. Guest perceives maximum luxury.
- **Paid add-on (EUR X/day):** Positions as premium rental alternative. Revenue center for hotel. But risks feeling transactional.
- **Complimentary for VIP / repeat guests, paid for others:** Tiered model. Complex to operate but maximizes both perception and revenue.

Each model has different implications for the hotel P&L, the guest experience, and the Ford relationship. The deck must pick one and defend it.

### What about guests who do not drive?

Not every UHNW guest drives. Many travel with private chauffeurs. Some are elderly. Some simply prefer to be driven. Is the Bronco available with a driver? Is there a chauffeur service component? If a guest cannot or will not drive, the entire proposition is irrelevant to them. This needs a fallback.

## 6. SLIDE-BY-SLIDE NOTES

### Slide 00 (Cover)
- Ford logo should not be the largest logo on the cover. Re-weight: Fouquet's and L'Officiel at parity with Ford, or Ford smaller.
- The Heritage Edition render is fine but a photograph would be stronger. Even a composite of a Bronco on an SBH road would feel more real than a configurator image.
- Add a date and a "Confidential" mark. This is a partnership proposal, not a public document.
- Add "Prepared for [recipient name]" -- personalization signals seriousness.

### Slide 01 (The Problem)
- Strong. Keep the core comparison.
- Remove "Zero content value" from the right column. Replace with "No aesthetic consideration" or "No brand alignment."
- The EUR 3,000/night figure should be verified for Fouquet's specifically. If their published rate is different, use the real number.

### Slide 02 (The Solution)
- "Fleet Luxe sources, imports, registers, insures, maintains, and rotates vehicles" -- this is an operations summary, not a value proposition. Lead with the guest experience: "A vehicle that belongs to the destination as much as the villa, the beach, and the sunset."
- Source / Operate / Amplify cards are fine but "Amplify" should be reframed for the hospitality audience. "Amplify" = marketing language. Try "Distinguish" or "Elevate" -- something about what the hotel gains, not what the content machine produces.

### Slide 03 (Why Bronco)
- "Ford is repositioning Bronco as feminine, fashion-forward, luxury" -- this is Ford's internal strategy language. A luxury audience does not need to know Ford's demographic goals. They need to see why the Heritage Edition is beautiful and appropriate for this landscape.
- "70% Buyers are male" -- delete. This is a Ford marketing stat. It has no place in a luxury hospitality pitch.
- "0 Caribbean activations" -- delete. This frames the opportunity in Ford's terms ("we can be first") rather than the guest's terms.
- "126 Heritage Editions sitting in US dealer inventory right now -- Nuke's data layer tracks every unit" -- this is internal operational capability. Impressive for an investor deck, inappropriate here. The audience does not care about your data platform in this context.
- The vehicle images from Ford's configurator are adequate but not compelling. Commission photography or at minimum use press images from the Hamptons activation for proof of concept.

### Slide 04 (The Placement)
- "Three Marsh Gray. One Desert Sand." -- Good. Specific.
- "A 2026 Ford Heritage fleet on a UNESCO-recognized beach, every day." -- Drop "2026 Ford Heritage fleet." Say "Heritage Edition Broncos on a UNESCO-recognized coastline." The model year and brand name clutter the poetry.
- This slide needs a hero image. Text on a green background is not a placement slide. Show a Bronco (even a composite) parked at Fouquet's or on Shell Beach.

### Slide 05 (Strategic Collaboration)
- Five partner cards in a row is visually cramped. Consider a 2+3 or 3+2 layout.
- L'Agence description is too vague: "PR, curation, client relationships. Ensures every touchpoint meets luxury standard." This must be specific or it signals that our role has not been thought through. Concrete version: "Manages client introductions, curates guest-vehicle pairing, coordinates media appearances, ensures brand consistency across all partner touchpoints."
- L'Agence has no logo in this deck (text fallback noted in the code). This is disrespectful. Get our logo or do not include us. A text placeholder next to real logos from Ford, Fouquet's, and L'Officiel makes us look like an afterthought.

### Slide 06 (The Ecosystem)
- The flow diagrams are clear and useful.
- "L'Agence ensures the client relationship stays flawless through every touchpoint" -- still too vague. What specific touchpoints? Pre-arrival briefing? Airport greeting? Vehicle orientation? Mid-stay check-in? Departure follow-up?
- Remove "Ford sees their 2026 Heritage fleet in daily use on the most exclusive island in the Caribbean" from the guest-facing narrative. This is Ford's reward, not the guest's experience.

### Slide 07 (FBM Deep Dive)
- Good operational detail. This is the right level for an operations-focused partner.
- "Security blanket -- if anything goes wrong, FBM is 5 minutes away" -- good. This is the kind of practical reassurance that hotel GMs need.
- The FBM card with "Airport Frontage" messaging is strong.

### Slide 08 (Competition)
- "Zero content value" appears again. Remove it everywhere.
- "SOGUAVA (Guadeloupe)" -- internal supply chain detail. The audience does not need to know who distributes Suzukis.
- The comparison format works. The substance needs refinement per the voice notes above.

### Slide 09 (Guest Journey)

### Slide 10 (Supply Chain)

### Slide 11 (Economics)

- Add a "Guest economics" section: what does the hotel charge the guest (or absorb into rate), and what is the hotel's ROI calculation?

### Slide 12 (Documentation)
- Appendix material. Move out of main deck.
- Useful for the operations team but has no place in a pitch to luxury partners.

### Slide 13 (Acquisition Paths)
- Same as 12. This is internal strategy, not a pitch slide.
- "VLVA (NV dealer, DLR000053625)" -- dealer license numbers in a luxury pitch deck. No.

### Slide 14 (Scale)
- "St. Barths is the proof of concept" -- good framing.
- The four destination categories (Caribbean, Mediterranean, Mountain, Desert) are aspirational and appropriate.

## 7. WHAT L'AGENCE NEEDS TO COMMIT

### Our deliverables (proposed):

1. **Client introductions** -- Open our network to Fleet Luxe for hotel partnerships. This is worth more than any other single contribution. Our relationships with Barriere, LVMH Hospitality, Oetker Collection, Belmond, and Rosewood are decades-deep. Access to these is not free.

2. **Media coordination** -- Manage press relationships for launch and ongoing coverage. Coordinate with L'Officiel editorial team. Pitch to international publications. Manage press trips.

3. **Event activation** -- Plan and execute launch event for fleet arrival. Coordinate Bucket Regatta presence. Manage New Year's week visibility.

4. **Guest experience design** -- Define the touchpoints: pre-arrival communication, airport handoff protocol, vehicle orientation, mid-stay check-in, departure feedback collection. This is the service design layer that makes Fleet Luxe feel like luxury rather than a rental.

5. **Brand governance** -- Ensure all materials, communications, and guest-facing touchpoints meet luxury standards. Review all content before publication. Approve all social media posts.

6. **Ongoing PR** -- Monthly media monitoring, quarterly press updates, annual program review.

### Fee structure (to be negotiated, but the deck must acknowledge this exists):

- **Retainer:** Monthly retainer for ongoing coordination, PR, and brand governance. EUR range to be discussed based on scope.
- **Introduction fee:** Per-hotel commission when L'Agence's network produces a new Fleet Luxe partnership.
- **Event fee:** Per-event management fee for activations.
- **Expense pass-through:** Media hosting, press materials, photography coordination.

### Exclusivity arrangement:

- L'Agence is the exclusive PR and concierge partner for Fleet Luxe in St. Barths and the broader Caribbean.
- No competing agency can represent Fleet Luxe in our territory.
- L'Agence has first right of refusal for Mediterranean expansion.
- If Fleet Luxe partners with a competing concierge agency in any of our active markets, the exclusivity clause triggers a termination option.

### What must be in the deck before we will present it:

1. Remove all dealer license numbers, tax form references, and internal operational details from the main narrative. Move to appendix.
2. Resolve the economics: pick Option A or B and present the annual total cost including operations.
3. Define L'Agence's role with specific deliverables, not adjectives.
4. Add a liability and insurance section.
5. Add a timeline with specific dates.
6. Remove all "content value" language from guest-facing sections.
7. Get our logo or remove our name from the brand row.
8. Add a call to action on the closing slide.
9. Add a media plan with named publications and activation events.
10. Build a second version optimized for the Ford audience (separate from the hospitality version).

## Summary

The bones are here. The problem statement is real. The operational homework is genuine. The partnership structure makes sense. But the deck is trying to speak to Ford, Fouquet's, L'Agence, and L'Officiel simultaneously, and it ends up speaking clearly to none of them.

Split it. Build the Ford deck and the hospitality deck separately. Define the ask. Define the economics. Define the timeline. Define L'Agence's scope and compensation. Remove the startup-pitch language from the luxury narrative. Then we can talk about showing it to our network.

Until then, this stays in the drafts folder.

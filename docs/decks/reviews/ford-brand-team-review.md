**Reviewer:** Bronco Experiential Marketing, Ford Motor Company

## 1. BRAND COMPLIANCE ISSUES

### Ford Logo Usage — REJECTED

The deck uses a Wikimedia Commons SVG (`upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3e/Ford_logo_flat.svg`) as the Ford logo. This is unacceptable. Ford provides approved brand assets through the Ford Brand Standards portal. No external party should be sourcing our logo from Wikipedia. This alone would get the deck rejected by legal before anyone reads slide 2.

**Required:** Request approved assets through Ford Brand Licensing. The Blue Oval has specific clear-space requirements, minimum size standards, and approved color variants (Ford Blue on white, white on Ford Blue, monochrome). The flat SVG used here does not match any approved variant.

### "Ford Antenna" Font Reference

The Nuke database references "Ford Antenna" as our primary typeface, which is correct — Antenna is our brand typeface. However, the deck itself uses Inter (Google Fonts). If this deck is being presented as a Ford co-branded document, it cannot use Inter. If it is a third-party proposal TO Ford, Inter is acceptable but the deck should not reference or attempt to replicate Ford typography. It currently does neither — it just ignores our type system entirely while storing our font metadata in its backend. Unclear what the intent is.

### Build.ford.com Configurator Images

The deck embeds live URLs from `build.ford.com/dig/Ford/Bronco/2026/HD-TILE...` — these are Ford's Build & Price configurator renders. These are NOT licensed for third-party presentations, pitch decks, or commercial proposals. They are served for the consumer configuration experience on ford.com.

**Required:** If Ford agrees to participate in any activation, we would provide approved press images, lifestyle photography, and product renders through Ford Media Center. Using our configurator API as an image source in a sales deck is a compliance violation.

### Heritage Edition Trademark

"Heritage Edition" is a Ford trim designation. The deck uses it freely throughout (slides 3, 4, 8, 13) without trademark attribution. Any external document referencing Ford trims must include appropriate trademark notices. The phrase "Heritage Edition" should appear as "Heritage Edition(TM)" on first usage, with a footnote: "Heritage Edition is a trademark of Ford Motor Company."

### Brand Hierarchy Violation

The deck places Ford as one of five equal "partners" (slide 5), positioned alongside a local car dealership (FBM), a hotel (Fouquet's), a regional magazine (L'Officiel St Barth), and a PR agency (L'Agence). Ford Motor Company is a $160 billion global automaker. We are not a "partner" in a five-way split with a Caribbean garage. If Ford participates, Ford is the brand principal. Everyone else is an activation partner or vendor.

## 2. BUSINESS CASE GAPS

### No ROI Model

The deck contains zero projections for:
- Impressions (earned media, social, press)
- Reach (audience size, demographic breakdown)
- Conversion metrics (consideration lift, dealer traffic, online configuration starts)
- Media value equivalent (what would this exposure cost through paid channels?)

Ford's experiential marketing team evaluates every activation against cost-per-impression and earned media value. The alice + olivia Hamptons activation had specific KPIs: social impressions, press mentions, configuration starts from QR codes on-site, and demographic shift in Bronco consideration among women 25-45. This deck has none of that.

### Unclear Ask

What is Ford being asked to do? The deck presents two options (slide 11):
- **Option A:** "Client pays ~$250-273K" — so Ford does nothing?
- **Option B:** "Ford provides at cost via brand activation budget. Client pays shipping + duties only (~$35K)" — so Ford eats $220K+ in vehicle cost?

Neither option is an actual proposal. Where is the co-op budget request? What is the dollar amount being requested from Ford? What does Ford get in return for that spend? What is the contract term? Is this a lease, a sale, a loan, a sponsorship? The economics slide reads like a cost estimate for the proposer, not a business case for Ford.

### No Comparable Activation Benchmarks

The deck mentions the alice + olivia collaboration once (slide 3) but provides no performance data from it or any other Ford activation. For context, Ford's experiential team needs to see:
- What similar activations cost per impression
- What the industry benchmark is for luxury automotive brand placements in hospitality
- What the proposer's track record is (how many activations have they executed?)

### No Attribution Methodology

How does Ford measure that a St. Barths guest who drove a Heritage Edition later bought a Bronco? There is no tracking mechanism proposed. No unique URLs, no QR codes, no CRM integration, no dealer attribution. The deck says guests will post on Instagram and "Ford sees it" — that is not a measurement framework.

## 3. DEALER & FRANCHISE LAW CONCERNS

### Ford Cannot Sell Directly

Ford operates through a franchised dealer network. Every vehicle sale must go through a licensed Ford dealer. The deck acknowledges this ("Ford franchise law — must go through a dealer") but treats it as a minor bottleneck rather than a structural requirement that shapes the entire deal.

### Which Dealer?

The deck says "US Dealer" generically and mentions "126 Heritage Editions in stock nationally." Which dealer is involved? Has any dealer agreed to participate? Ford's brand team can recommend a dealer but cannot compel one. We need a named dealer with a letter of intent.

The deck mentions "VLVA (NV dealer, DLR000053625)" as a fallback — is this a real Ford dealer? What is their franchise agreement status? Do they have export experience? Have they been contacted? The inclusion of a specific dealer license number suggests some research was done, but there is no confirmation of dealer participation.

### Export Compliance

Exporting new Ford vehicles to a French overseas collectivity (St. Barths operates under EU/French regulatory frameworks with local exceptions) raises compliance questions:
- Does the vehicle meet EU/French emissions and safety standards, or does St. Barths have exemptions?
- Who handles the NHTSA export filing?
- Ford's territory agreements with SOGUAVA (mentioned on slide 10 as the current Ford distributor in Guadeloupe, which covers St. Barths) — does this activation conflict with SOGUAVA's territorial rights?

The deck mentions SOGUAVA with the note "May need to bypass." You do not "bypass" a Ford authorized distributor. SOGUAVA holds territorial distribution rights. Any Ford vehicles entering their territory need to be coordinated with them, not routed around them. This is a legal issue, not a logistics issue.

### ST-4 Tax Path

The deck references Georgia Form ST-4 (dealer-to-dealer tax exemption) as part of the VLVA intermediary path. This is a state tax form for intra-dealer transfers. Using it for what is functionally an export sale to a foreign entity through a Nevada intermediary would need review by Ford's tax counsel. We do not want our brand associated with a transaction structure that could be characterized as tax avoidance.

## 4. COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE

### What Competitors Are Doing in Caribbean Luxury

The deck does not address the competitive landscape from the automaker's perspective. It compares Heritage Broncos to Suzuki Jimnys and Mokes — that is the rental car comparison, not the brand activation comparison.

**What Ford needs to know:**

- **Mercedes-Benz:** The G-Wagon is already the default luxury beach vehicle in St. Barths. G63 AMGs are parked outside Eden Rock daily. Mercedes does not need an activation — they have organic presence. How does a Heritage Edition Bronco compete with organic G-Wagon saturation?

- **Land Rover / Range Rover:** The natural luxury-off-road competitor. Has Land Rover done hospitality activations in the Caribbean? (Answer: yes — Range Rover has placed vehicles at Sandy Lane in Barbados and at COMO Parrot Cay in Turks & Caicos.) The deck does not acknowledge this.

- **Jeep / Stellantis:** Wrangler is the obvious Bronco competitor. Jeep has a natural Caribbean presence through rental fleets. Has Stellantis done any curated hotel activations? The deck does not say.

- **Porsche:** The Cayenne is ubiquitous in St. Barths. Porsche has done experiential activations at luxury hotels globally (Porsche Drive program). Are they in the Caribbean?

The competitive slide (slide 8) compares "Mokes & Suzukis" to Heritage Broncos. This is comparing a rental car to a brand activation. The real question is: how does Ford Bronco position against the G-Wagon, Range Rover, and Cayenne that are already organically present on the island? The deck avoids this question entirely.

### Heritage Edition vs. G63 AMG in Ultra-Luxury Context

A Heritage Edition Bronco MSRPs at ~$59,500. A G63 AMG starts at ~$180,000. In the ultra-luxury context of St. Barths ($3,000/night hotels, private helicopters), the Heritage Edition is positioned as an accessible premium product, not an ultra-luxury one. The deck needs to address this directly: is the play "charming heritage character" rather than "luxury price parity"? If so, lean into it. But do not pretend a $60K Bronco competes with a $200K G-Wagon on status signaling. The Heritage Edition's advantage would be uniqueness and nostalgia — own that positioning.

## 5. CONTENT & MEDIA REQUIREMENTS

### Content Approval Rights

Ford requires pre-approval of all branded content before publication. Any photography, video, or social media content featuring Ford vehicles with visible branding must go through Ford's content review process. The deck mentions L'Officiel St Barth as the media partner but says nothing about Ford's content approval rights.

**Required:** A content approval clause giving Ford review and veto rights on all published materials featuring the Bronco or Ford brand marks.

### Photography & Video Deliverables

What specifically will Ford receive? The deck says "every vehicle is content" but does not specify:
- Number of professional photo/video shoots
- Deliverable format (resolution, aspect ratios, usage rights)
- Shoot frequency (weekly? monthly? per guest?)
- Who pays for production?

### Content Ownership & Usage Rights

Ford requires global, perpetual, royalty-free usage rights for any content produced as part of a brand activation. Who owns the content? L'Officiel? The hotel? The guest? Fleet Luxe? Ford needs to be able to use activation content across our own channels (ford.com, social, advertising, dealer materials) without restriction.

### Social Media Guidelines

Ford has strict social media guidelines. Which handles will be used to post activation content? Will it be:
- @Ford / @FordBronco (our handles — requires our social team's involvement)
- @LOfficielStBarth (their handle — requires content approval)
- @FouquetsStBarth (hotel handle — same)
- Guest handles (user-generated — different rules)

The deck does not address social media governance at all.

## 6. WHAT FORD NEEDS TO SEE IN THE NEXT VERSION

If this proposal is to advance beyond initial review, the next version must include:

1. **Clear ask slide:** What exactly is Ford being asked to provide? Vehicles? Money? Both? For how long? Under what terms?

2. **Impressions projections:** Estimated earned media impressions with methodology. Who is the audience? What is the projected reach across social, press, and organic channels?

3. **Media value estimate:** Dollar-equivalent value of the exposure Ford would receive. Compare to what equivalent paid media would cost.

4. **Dealer commitment letter:** A named Ford dealer who has agreed to facilitate the transaction, with export experience documented.

5. **Insurance coverage proof:** Fleet insurance documentation showing adequate coverage for four $60K+ vehicles in a Caribbean environment (hurricanes, salt air, narrow roads, tourist drivers).

6. **Content usage agreement:** Draft agreement granting Ford global usage rights for all activation content.

7. **SOGUAVA coordination plan:** Evidence that Ford's existing distributor in the territory has been contacted and is either participating or has provided a waiver.

8. **Measurement framework:** How will success be measured? Define KPIs before the activation, not after.

9. **Competitive positioning:** Honest assessment of how Heritage Edition Bronco fits into an island where G-Wagons and Range Rovers are already present.

10. **Approved Ford assets:** Replace the Wikimedia logo with Ford-provided brand assets. Remove the build.ford.com configurator images.

## 7. RED FLAGS

### Immediate "No" Triggers

1. **Wikimedia Ford logo.** No legitimate Ford partner would source our logo from Wikipedia. This signals the proposer has no existing relationship with Ford. It would be embarrassing if this deck reached anyone senior in Dearborn with a Wikipedia-sourced Blue Oval on the cover.

2. **"May need to bypass" SOGUAVA.** Suggesting we bypass our own authorized distributor is a non-starter. This shows a misunderstanding of how Ford's distribution network operates. SOGUAVA is not an obstacle — they are a Ford business partner with contractual rights.

3. **No named dealer, no named Ford contact.** This appears to be an unsolicited proposal from an entity with no existing Ford relationship. There is no indication that anyone at Ford has been contacted, let alone expressed interest. The deck assumes Ford will participate without establishing that any conversation has occurred.

4. **Build.ford.com images used without authorization.** Using Ford's proprietary configurator renders in a commercial proposal is a licensing violation. This needs to be removed immediately regardless of whether the proposal advances.

5. **Nuke "tracks every unit, every color, every dealer."** Slide 3 claims "126 Heritage Editions sitting in US dealer inventory right now — Nuke's data layer tracks every unit, every color, every dealer." If an outside entity is scraping Ford's dealer inventory data, that raises data governance questions. This is not something to advertise in a pitch deck to Ford.

### What Needs to Be Removed Before Sending to Dearborn

- The Wikimedia Ford logo (replace with placeholder or request approved assets)
- The build.ford.com configurator images (replace with publicly available press images from Ford Media Center)
- The "may need to bypass" SOGUAVA language
- The Nuke inventory tracking claim (or reframe as "publicly available dealer search data")
- The VLVA dealer license number (do not put specific dealer credentials in an unsolicited pitch)
- Any suggestion that Ford is a co-equal "partner" in a five-way arrangement

## 3 THINGS THAT WOULD MAKE ME TAKE A MEETING

Despite the compliance issues, this deck has three elements that are genuinely interesting:

1. **The gap is real.** The observation that ultra-luxury hospitality curates everything except the vehicle is accurate. We have heard this from our own brand ambassadors. The Hamptons activation with alice + olivia worked precisely because it placed Bronco in a lifestyle context where the vehicle was part of the experience, not just transportation. St. Barths is an even more concentrated version of that market. The problem statement on slide 1 is strong.

2. **FBM as operational backbone.** Most brand activation proposals hand-wave the logistics. This one has a named local operator (FBM Automobiles) with a physical location at the airport, an existing service center, and apparent local experience with import/registration. If FBM is real and capable, that solves the hardest part of Caribbean vehicle placement. The operational slide (slide 7) is the strongest in the deck.

3. **Heritage Edition is the right product.** The Heritage Edition's retro design language, limited-run positioning, and color palette (Marsh Gray, Desert Sand) genuinely align with the St. Barths aesthetic better than a standard Bronco. Someone on this team understands the product. The visual match between Heritage Edition design cues and Caribbean luxury is not accidental — it is a legitimate brand insight.

---

**Bottom line:** The concept has merit. The execution is not ready for Ford. Fix the compliance issues, build a real business case with measurable KPIs, establish contact with SOGUAVA, secure a dealer commitment, and come back with a deck that treats Ford as the brand principal rather than one partner among five. If those things happen, this gets a meeting.

**Next step:** If the proposer wants to advance this, they should contact Ford's Experiential Marketing team through official channels or work through a Ford-authorized agency of record. Unsolicited decks with Wikipedia logos do not make it past the first filter.

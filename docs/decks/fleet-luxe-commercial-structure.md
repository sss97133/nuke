# Luxe Fleet — Real Commercial Structure

## THE THESIS

St. Barths is where people and things go to be seen and validated. The Hermès boutique in Gustavia is the highest-selling Hermès boutique in the world. Eden Rock is the most recognized hotel destination in the Caribbean. Every luxury brand that matters has a presence on this island — not because of transaction volume, but because being seen there validates the brand at a global level.

The automotive experience on SBH is undercurated. Not because there's no demand — FBM has sold ~20 Broncos through painful back-channels. It's because no automotive brand has recognized the value of the real estate. The island is flooded with Jimnys and Mokes and Chinese buggies because rental companies are answering the call on margins — negotiating $1,500 commissions vs $2,000 commissions. They optimize for the transaction.

**The heritage paradox:** St. Barths protects everything. The beaches are UNESCO. You can't build new structures — only restore existing ones. The villas are preserved to period accuracy. The stone walls on the road to Colombier are heritage infrastructure. The vegetation, the coastline, the architecture — all curated, all protected. But the automotive landscape is completely unregulated. Anyone can flood it with whatever cheap inventory yields the best margin. A guest in a heritage villa wearing Loro Piana gets into a Kia Picanto. The villa was restored to spec. The linen was sourced from Italy. The vehicle was sourced from whoever offered the lowest commission.

The role that's actually needed — open-top 4x4, surfboard hanging out, coast road to Saline — is being filled by scooters. Not because scooters are right, but because nobody showed up with the right vehicle. The Bronco doesn't need to dominate. It needs to fill a role that's currently vacant. Heritage Edition on SBH isn't an American invasion — it's the right object in the right landscape. The same preservation instinct that protects the stone walls and the rooflines is the instinct that should govern what drives on the roads.

The insight: **Ford's experiential marketing budget for a single activation outweighs the entire cost of all vehicle sales on the entire island.** The rental companies see vehicles. We see media real estate. The automotive landscape on SBH is left to people who think in commissions because nobody from the brand side has shown up to think in impressions.

Hermès didn't open in Gustavia because they'd sell more bags there than in Paris. They opened there because every bag sold in Gustavia is seen by the right person, photographed in the right light, and validates the brand at a scale no advertising can match. Ford should think about SBH the same way Hermès does.

### Historical Precedent: Hertz x Ford

The Shelby GT350-H (1966) and subsequent Hertz-Ford partnerships are THE precedent:
- Ford placed Mustang GT350s in the Hertz rental fleet
- Renters fell in love with the car during their rental period
- Hertz became a purchase funnel — people rented a Mustang, then bought one
- The "Rent-a-Racer" program became legendary: experiential product placement that drove sales
- Ford has done this before. SBH is the luxury version of the same thesis.
- Modern version: Hertz ordered 100,000 Teslas (2021) after seeing rental demand signal purchase intent

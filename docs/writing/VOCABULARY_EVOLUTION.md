| Mar 2026 | 1,392 | 819 | 5.6% | 39.0% | 22.1% | 12.6 words |

### What Changed

The average prompt length decreased from 855 to 819 characters over the 141-day period. Structured prompts (containing markdown headers, bullet lists, code fences, or tables) rose from 0.9% to 39.0%. Meanwhile, terse prompts (under 50 characters) went from 45.5% to 22.1%. Profanity rate dropped from 7.1% to 5.6%.

This arc reflects a transition from exploratory, conversational prompting to specification-driven engineering. Early prompts were often quick stream-of-consciousness commands ("fix the homepage why isn't it showing"). By February and March, prompts increasingly arrived as structured briefs with headers, acceptance criteria, and explicit constraints -- the user had learned what information the model needed to produce good output, and was front-loading that context. The vocabulary shift (Section II) mirrors this: informal verbs like "scrape" gave way to precise domain terms like "observe," reflecting both growing domain expertise and a more deliberate communication style.

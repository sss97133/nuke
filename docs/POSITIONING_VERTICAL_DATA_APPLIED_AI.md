# Positioning: Vertical Data + Applied AI

**Use this when you need to explain what Nuke is in one smart sentence — without sounding like a horizontal infra play or an "AI wrapper."**

---

## The one-sentence version

**We're vertical data + applied AI: we own the data layer for a specific industry (collector vehicles → hands-on trades), and we run AI on top of that data to do a concrete job (find opportunities, price work, match humans). We're not horizontal training infra and we're not a thin wrapper on someone else's data.**

---

## Why "vertical data" matters

- **Horizontal** = tools that work for any domain (e.g. "databases for ML," "dev environments for training"). Everyone’s potential customer; no one’s default answer for "who owns this domain?"
- **Vertical** = we picked a slice of the world (vehicles, then trades) and built the data and intelligence **for that slice**. We’re the place that knows collector cars and repair economics.

So when someone says "are you building training infra?" the answer is: **No. We’re building the data and applied intelligence for a vertical. Training happens inside our stack; we don’t sell training tools.**

---

## Why "applied AI" (and not "AI-powered" or "AI wrapper")

- **Applied AI** = AI is in the product to do a specific job: identify undervalued assets, estimate repair ROI, score reliability, match jobs to people. The value is the **outcome** (better decisions, fewer broken deals), not "we called an API."
- **AI wrapper** = thin layer on someone else’s model + someone else’s data. We’re the opposite: we own the pipelines, the schema, the models, and the distribution. The AI is applied **to our data** to produce **our outputs**.

So: **We apply AI to our own vertical data to automate discovery, pricing, and matching. We’re not a wrapper.**

---

## How to say it out loud (conversation-ready)

**Short:**  
"Vertical data plus applied AI. We own the data layer for collector vehicles and hands-on trades, and we run AI on it to find opportunities, price work, and match jobs to people — not horizontal training tools, not an AI wrapper."

**If they ask "what does that mean?":**  
"We picked a vertical — cars, then trades — and built the data infrastructure and intelligence for it. So we’re not selling 'ML databases' or 'training infra' to everyone; we’re the data + AI stack for this niche. The AI is applied to do a job: find undervalued cars, estimate repair ROI, vet tradespeople. We own the data and the models; the product is the outcomes."

**If they say "so you’re B2B data / infra?":**  
"Yes — B2B data infrastructure for a vertical. The infra is the data layer and the applied AI on top. Our customers get structured intelligence and decisions, not raw tooling."

---

## What we’re *not* (and why that’s good)

- **Not horizontal training infra** — We’re not "making LLM/training easy for everyone." We make *our* training and models easy for *our* product. Different category, different buyers.
- **Not an AI wrapper** — We own extraction, schema, pipelines, and models. The value is in the data and the applied intelligence, not in wrapping a single API.
- **Not generic "AI for X"** — We’re specifically data + applied AI for a vertical. That’s a clear, fundable category: vertical ownership + concrete outcomes.

You can keep using "data layer for niche hands-on professions" and "bots that hire humans" as the hook; "vertical data + applied AI" is the crisp way to place that in the market so it doesn’t get confused with training infra or wrappers.

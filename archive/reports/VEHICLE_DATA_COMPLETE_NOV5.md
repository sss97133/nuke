## Key Insights

### Pre-1981 VIN Decoding
Critical discovery: Pre-1981 GM VINs use a different year encoding:
- **Position 5** (not position 4) contains the year
- **'8' = 1968** (not 1978 as in modern VINs)
- **'9' = 1969**, **'0' = 1970**, **'1' = 1971**, etc.

This corrected a major misidentification of classic trucks.

### GM Truck Designations (1960s-1980s)
- **C = 2WD** (Conventional)
- **K = 4WD** (4x4)
- **V = 4WD** (GMC variant, 1980s)
- **10 = 1/2 ton**, **20 = 3/4 ton**, **30 = 1 ton**
- **3+3 = Crew cab** (with dually often)

### Common Engine Combinations
- **1967-1987 GM Trucks:** 350 V8 (5.7L) most common
- **3/4 ton and larger:** 400ci or 454ci for heavy duty
- **Late 1960s-early 1970s:** 396 big block for performance
- **1985+:** 6.2L diesel option for heavy duty

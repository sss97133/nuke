# YC Video Storyboard -- 60 Seconds
## "The Data Layer" -- Roots-style voiceover

Shot on phone. Raw. No polish. The authenticity IS the production value.

---

### SHOT 1 -- THE OPEN (0:00 - 0:08)
**Visual:** You driving your truck through Boulder City. Desert, open road, morning light. Dash cam angle or phone mounted on dash. Engine hum underneath.

**VO:** "Every car has a story. The problem is, nobody wrote it down. It's in auction comments. Forum posts from 2008. Some guy's Instagram story that disappeared yesterday."

---

### SHOT 2 -- THE SHOP (0:08 - 0:16)
**Visual:** Pull into your shop. Cut to hands-on shots -- tools on a bench, a vehicle up on a lift or parts laid out. The real thing.

**VO:** "I work on trucks. I build things with my hands. And I kept hitting the same wall -- trying to find the real history on a vehicle and realizing it doesn't exist. Not in any database. It's scattered across the internet in a thousand places nobody's connecting."

---

### SHOT 3 -- THE SCREEN (0:16 - 0:24)
**Visual:** Cut to your phone or laptop screen. Quick montage: the Nuke dashboard, vehicle profiles, the feed with 758K vehicles, data flowing. Keep it fast -- 2-3 screen grabs, 2 seconds each. Maybe show a BaT listing with 300 comments on one side, your structured extraction on the other.

**VO:** "So I built Nuke. Throw anything at it -- a listing, a forum post, a photo, a text message. It handles the sorting. Structures it. Links it. Makes it usable."

---

### SHOT 4 -- THE SCALE (0:24 - 0:32)
**Visual:** Screen recordings or stills: the numbers. 758K vehicles. 627K observations. 132K BaT listings. The Telegram interface showing extraction running. The Feb 3rd session -- "1,000 complete" in the morning, "140,826 complete" by evening. Quick cuts.

**VO:** "758,000 vehicles. 627,000 data points. 181 extraction tools I built by hand. One day in February I ran the whole system from my phone while I was underneath a Silverado. Went from a thousand extractions to a hundred and forty thousand by dinner."

---

### SHOT 5 -- THE THROWBACKS (0:32 - 0:42)
**Visual:** Quick montage of the "shit you've seen" -- the wildest vehicles, auction screenshots, forum gold, absurd listings, data corruption you caught, before/after of messy data → clean structured output. Old photos of builds, wrenching, car meets. The journey. 3-4 shots, fast cuts.

**VO:** "I've seen every corner of this world. The forums. The auctions. The parking lot deals. The $200,000 cars with no paperwork and the $2,000 trucks with a shoebox full of receipts. All of it is data. Nobody's organizing it. Until now."

---

### SHOT 6 -- THE CLOSE (0:42 - 0:52)
**Visual:** Back to you. Standing in the shop or leaning against your truck. Talking to camera for the first time. Direct. No bullshit.

**VO (to camera):** "I'm [name]. Nuke is the data layer for hands-on professions. We start with vehicles because I know this world. But the pattern is the same everywhere -- watches, boats, bikes, any trade where the knowledge lives in people's heads and comment sections instead of databases. We structure it. We make it usable."

---

### SHOT 7 -- THE TAG (0:52 - 0:60)
**Visual:** You getting back in the truck, or walking back into the shop. Maybe a wide shot of Boulder City in the background. Cut to Nuke logo or URL on screen.

**VO:** "The people who know the most about these machines are the ones who work on them. I'm building the tool so they don't have to be data scientists too."

*Engine starts. Cut to black. URL.*

---

## Production Notes

- **Total: 60 seconds**
- **Shoot on your phone.** YC explicitly says they don't care about production quality. The rawness matches the pitch.
- **The truck and shop ARE the pitch.** You're not in a WeWork. You're in a garage in Boulder City building data infrastructure between oil changes. That's the whole story.
- **Screen recordings:** Capture 30 seconds of the Nuke dashboard, the Telegram interface, and the Feb 3rd session timestamps before you shoot. You'll cut these in over the voiceover.
- **Throwback photos:** Pull the wildest stuff from your camera roll. Auctions, builds, wrenching, car meets, the Silverado. These flash by fast during shot 5.
- **Music:** Optional. If anything, something low and sparse. The truck engine and shop ambient noise might be enough.
- **Edit:** iMovie or CapCut. Cut tight. No transitions, just hard cuts. Match the pace of the voiceover.
- **Upload as unlisted to YouTube.** Not private -- unlisted. YC can't see private videos.
